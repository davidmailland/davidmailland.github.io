Selected publications

From Mathematics to Art: Modelling the Pascal’s Triangle with Petri Nets

Published in Symmetry (Special Issue), 2025

We propose a Petri-net-based modelling of Pascal’s triangle, bridging discrete mathematics and formal modelling. The approach supports structural insights and visually interpretable representations.

Recommended citation:
Mailland, D.; Grobelna, I. (2025). From Mathematics to Art: Modelling the Pascal's Triangle with Petri Nets. Symmetry, 17(12), 2181. DOI: 10.3390/sym17122181

A Novel Approach to the Collatz Conjecture with Petri Nets

Published in Information (MDPI), 2025

We propose a Petri-net-based modelling of the inverse dynamics of the Collatz conjecture. The construction reveals a structured tree of predecessors up to a bounded domain and allows the study of reachability, liveness, and recurrence properties using formal methods.

Recommended citation:
Mailland, D.; Grobelna, I. (2025). A Novel Approach to the Collatz Conjecture with Petri Nets. Information, 16(9), 745. DOI: 10.3390/info16090745

Design of Automotive HMI: New Challenges in Enhancing User Experience, Safety, and Security

Published in Applied Sciences (MDPI), 2025

This narrative review addresses recent developments and future challenges in the design of automotive human–machine interfaces (HMIs), with a focus on user experience, safety, and security. It synthesises contemporary research on usability, emerging technologies, and interface design principles to support more intuitive interaction in advanced vehicles.

Recommended citation:
Grobelna, I.; Mailland, D.; Horwat, M. (2025). Design of Automotive HMI: New Challenges in Enhancing User Experience, Safety, and Security. Applied Sciences, 15(10), 5572. DOI: 10.3390/app15105572

Conference paper

Model Based Approach for RAMS Analyses in the Space Domain with Capella Open-Source Tool

Published in International Symposium on Model-Based Safety and Assessment (IMBSA 2019), 2019

This conference paper evaluates the applicability of a model-based approach to support dependability engineers performing RAMS (Reliability, Availability, Maintainability, Safety) analyses in the space domain using the open-source Capella tool, based on the Arcadia methodology. The approach aims to improve co-engineering activities during spacecraft design and explores preliminary applications and future improvements.

Recommended citation:
Bitetti, L.; De Ferluc, R.; Mailland, D.; Gregoris, G.; Capogna, F. (2019). Model Based Approach for RAMS Analyses in the Space Domain with Capella Open-Source Tool. In: Model-Based Safety and Assessment, Lecture Notes in Computer Science, vol. 11842, pp. 18–31. DOI: 10.1007/978-3-030-32872-6_2

Conference papers (in French)

Expérimentation de la méthodologie MBSA au domaine spatial

Published in Lambda-Mu 22 — 22e Congrès de Maîtrise des Risques et de Sûreté de Fonctionnement (Le Havre, e-congrès), 2020

Cet article présente une expérimentation de la méthodologie MBSA (Model-Based Safety Assessment) appliquée au domaine spatial. Les études menées au sein de Thales Alenia Space, en collaboration avec Ligeron, évaluent l’intérêt de cette approche pour les analyses de sûreté de fonctionnement et discutent les limites actuelles des outils basés sur AltaRica.

Recommended citation:
Bilba, T.; Mailland, D.; Dumont, L.; Schaff, M. (2020). Expérimentation de la méthodologie MBSA au domaine spatial. Lambda-Mu 22 — 22e Congrès de Maîtrise des Risques et de Sûreté de Fonctionnement, Le Havre (e-congrès), France. HAL Id: hal-03483601, v1.
