This page gathers visual explorations arising from mathematical structures. The emphasis is on representations that sit at the intersection of formal modelling, discrete mathematics, and visual intuition.

Fibonacci Recurrence and an Emergent Fractal-Like Figure

Starting from the Petri-net viewpoint developed for Pascal’s triangle, we apply an analogous local construction to the Fibonacci recurrence. The resulting parity-driven geometry produces a striking, fractal-like pattern.

To the best of our knowledge, this particular emergent figure has not been previously studied in the literature in this form, and it illustrates how simple firing rules can generate unexpected large-scale organisation.

Pascal’s Triangle as a Petri Net

This figure shows a Petri-net representation of Pascal’s triangle. Each place corresponds to a binomial coefficient, and the colour encodes its parity: black nodes represent odd values, white nodes even ones.

This parity colouring reveals the well-known Sierpiński-type structure embedded in Pascal’s triangle, directly induced by the local firing rules of the Petri net.
